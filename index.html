Unumployment Rate (%) Worldwide

Introduction

This visualization is about the Unemployment Rate (%). Generally, waves of unemployment occur during periods of rapid population growth, when the population is undereducated, or when the education system is unable to provide the right talent for the market. This visualiztion will give you a brief introduction to Unemployment Rate Worldwide.

Average Unemployment Rate (%) from 1991 - 2021

This visualization shows the average unemployment rate (%) over past 30 years (from 1991 - 2021). It provides an overview of the unemployment level worldwide. From the visualization, we can easily tell that some small countries has higher rate than others.

Unemployment Rate (%) Worldwide （1991 - 2021）

This visualization provides detail information about countries' unemployment rate in the past 30 years (1991 - 2021). Please feel free to explore the country region and time of your interest.

Unemployment Rate (%) Curve

It is clear that the trend in global unemployment rates declined significantly in 2008. There was a global financial crisis that year, which may have been one of the reasons why people were reluctant to leave their jobs. Similarly, there was a significant decline in global unemployment during the COVID-19 outbreak in 2019. People seem to be reluctant to leave their positions during times of disaster in order to prevent themselves from losing their financial resources and economic stability.
